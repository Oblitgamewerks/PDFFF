Level 1: Ghetto Fumigation                                          HOME



Lore:
The campaign opens in the immediate aftermath of a brutal liquidation, where the fires of resistance have just been violently extinguished. Emerging from the foul, subterranean sewer network, the player steps into the desolate outskirts of a recently cleared Jewish ghetto. The environment is a stark monument to Nazi atrocities—characterized by ruined urban architecture, collapsing brick tenements, and streets choked with heavy rubble and the burnt-out hulls of Panzer tanks. Active fires still consume what remains of the barricades, casting long shadows across the debris.

Fascist pigs:
Adding to the horror of this oppressive landscape, squads of Nazi soldiers can be seen picking through the structural decay, callously sorting through the smoking rubble and looted homes to strip the dead and displaced of any remaining spoils of war. This is a haunted battlefield of total destruction, where the smoke of a destroyed community still hangs thick in the air, marking the grim starting point of your mission.

Ambience:
The environment is bathed in a cold, light blue-tinged glow of early morning dawn, casting an eerie, clinical pallor over the devastation. This harsh, pale lighting cuts through the rising smoke, making the ruined urban architecture, collapsing brick tenements, and rubble-choked streets feel utterly exposed
yet deeply claustrophobic. It is a stark, tense, and desolate atmosphere.  

Audio Tone:
The audio design is  driven by a suspenseful, minimalist score. Low, droning cello notes and metallic thuds build a constant undercurrent of dread, mimicking a racing heartbeat. This heavy music is punctuated only by hyper-realistic environmental audio: the distant, hollow echo of a solitary gunshot, the guttural shouts of German patrols. The lack of traditional battlefield bombast creates an isolating, high-tension atmosphere where every sound is a potential threat.

Gameplay & Combat Loop:
This level serves as the formal baptism into the game’s core combat loop, immediately punishing complacency and discarding traditional shooter tropes. Rather than allowing players to rely on standard, linear head-on cover, the ruined urban architecture forces a deadly lesson in verticality and sharp situational awareness.

The ghetto's collapsing tenements are multi-level sniper nests; hostile forces are strategically positioned in fractured upper-story windows, crumbling balconies, and across jagged rooftops. This design forces the player to constantly scan the vertical horizon and adapt to overlapping, multi-directional fields of fire.

To survive, you must fluidly transition between aggressive close-quarters clearing of looting nazi scum and high-stakes firefights against fascist vermin holding the high ground. Enemies establish a relentless, high-tension rhythm where threats can rain down from any angle.







Level 2: The Pigs of War















 
Lore:
Beyond the ruined city streets lies The Iron Veins, a suffocating trench network hastily dug by the German Army during the final, desperate retreat of 1945. Originally designed as a last-ditch defensive line to stall the Allied advance, these claustrophobic, dirt-walled corridors have become a subterranean fortress for the Schwurbrüder—a cell of fanatical Nazi loyalists who have sworn a blood oath to fight to the absolute dead end.  

Stripped of air support and heavy armor, these soldiers have turned the labyrinthine fortifications into a meat grinder, utilizing hidden murder holes, subterranean tunnels, and blind corners they know by heart. In this dark, narrow hell, the open sky is replaced by barbed wire and paranoia; survival requires checking every shadow, as the garrison would rather cave the trenches in on themselves than yield an inch of ground.

Trigger Matrix & Combat Flow:
As one of the very first operational zones designed for the tactical campaign, the pacing throughout this canyon sector is deliberate, highly structured, and manageable for a focused vanguard. Simple entry into a designated canyon sector immediately alerts the local defensive troops, who respond with violent, coordinated suppressive fire.

Once you cross a major geological threshold or breach a reinforced checkpoint, a specific squad or entrenched defensive encampment is triggered to immediately engage your position, turning the entire map into a calculated series of tight, isolated skirmishes rather than an overwhelming,
unmanageable map-wide chaotic brawl.








Trench Trap:
The serpentine design of the fortified trenches and deep rock fissures severely restricts forward sightlines, compressing the combat space and forcing immediate, close-quarters engagement. This lack of visual clearance creates a high-tension environment where every blind corner presents an immediate ambush risk, requiring constant tactical vigilance. Furthermore, the vertical architecture introduces significant exposure; overhead wooden scaffolding allows enemy counter-snipers to establish dominant high-angle vantage points.

To survive, players must continuously monitor both immediate horizontal turns and the elevated structures above, balancing the threat of localized crossfire with sudden vertical attacks.

Even the natural desert environment itself functions as an active combat hazard, with seasoned shock troops hiding just behind the tangled messes of rusted barbed wire scattered throughout the trench network to catch moving targets off-guard. In a treacherous place like this, countless lethal hazards are always waiting to punish a reckless advance, making it so that death is effortless while survival demands every single shred of focus and momentum you have left in the tank.

Orwellian Propaganda:
Brightly colored propaganda posters, military banners, and signs are scattered everywhere across the facility walls, serving as a lethal, intentional visual distraction designed to break your focus in the heat of exploration. While you are busy reading a sign or attempting to decode a sector marker, a patrolling Nazi soldier will use the lapse in concentration to sneak up on your flank or round the immediate corner to blow you away before you can raise your weapon. Most of these signs feature aggressive psychological triggers, grandiosely advertising the various twisted, fascist ministries of the collapsing Nazi government while demanding absolute obedience to the state. They possess a distinctly grim, Orwellian look and aesthetic flavor that would make any authoritarian dictator proud, weaponizing the architecture itself to keep invaders disoriented, paranoid, and perpetually vulnerable.

Environmental Storytelling:
Beneath the violence, the trenches are packed with rich environmental storytelling assets that paint a bizarre picture of daily life for a garrison waiting on the edge of doom. Tucked into carved rock alcoves and sandbagged dugouts are functional, brightly lit vending machines, including retro cigarette machines, carbonated pop machines and mechanical hot dog machines still warming stale rations for the troops.

These strange, domestic details stand in contrast to the military hardware, offering a chilling glimpse into the mundane lives of the fanatical zealots rotting in their holes.
























Level 3: Desert of Stolen Souls

Antagonist:
Standartenführer Dietrich Von Schatz, Commander of the Sonderkommando Edelweiss (The Edelweiss Special Command). A fiercely loyal, fanatical tactician hand-picked by the inner circle to guard Hitler’s emergency lifeline. Von Schatz views this desolate canyon outpost as the literal birthplace of the future Reich, executing any staff member who shows even a shred of doubt regarding their final directive







Catalyst:
The Reichstreuhand-Koffer (The Reich Trust Chest). A staggering stash of three hundred million Reichsmarks in uncut, blood-stained diamonds plundered from across occupied Europe. This concentrated hoard of wealth acts as Hitler's personal, classified insurance policy, designed
specifically to fund a government-in-exile and buy political asylum in South America,
if the home front collapses entirely.

Site:
Altschlossfelsen Outpost (The Old Castle Rocks). A highly classified, concentric canyon maze naturally carved into the scorched, sun-baked desert rock of a hidden German border region.
The entire facility is built around a towering, heavily fortified sandstone plateau that houses a clandestine airfield, underground hangars, and a secure deep-bunker complex designed to
withstand heavy aerial bombardment.

Situation:
Berlin is actively crumbling, and Allied Intelligence has just flagged a massive spike in encrypted radio traffic radiating from a dead zone in the desert. The Fuhrer’s personal escape transport is currently fueled on the tarmac, its engines idling as the final logistics are ironed out. The uneven, dry riverbed leading directly into the canyon complex is a structural death trap, heavily salted with high-explosive ordnance and surrounded by crumbling, unstable cliff edges engineered to collapse under heavy weight. Allied Command has issued an absolute directive: you must penetrate the canyon network, systematically neutralize the fanatical Nazi garrison protecting the immediate airspace, and seize the emergency war chest before the transport aircraft can slip away into the clouds.

Directives:
Navigate the treacherous, mine-laden ravine without succumbing to the crumbling perimeter cliffs. Infiltrate the concentric canyon maze and bypass the outer security checkpoints. Neutralize the fanatical Standartenführer Dietrich Von Schatz along with his specialized defense garrison. Secure the airfield tarmac and seize the three hundred million Reichsmarks in stolen blood diamonds before the transport launches.

Dynamic Minefields:
The open terrain of the dry riverbed is a minefield where explosive charges are buried beneath shifting sands. Stepping off the established path triggers a sharp, metallic click, giving the player a split-second to dodge the directional blast before the ordnance detonates.


Crumbling Cliff Edges:
The upper paths overlooking the ravine are structurally unsound. Putting too much sustained pressure on the outer rock lips causes the sandstone to break away entirely, sending the player plunging into the jagged rocks below for an instant death if they fail to maintain forward momentum.








The Base Logistics:
The Perimeter Rifle: Placed right at the outer threshold of the canyon approach is a high-power bolt-action sniper rifle left behind by a scout team, designed specifically to let the player pick off the initial wave of airfield lookouts from extreme distance.

Dead-End Rewards:
Exploring the highly dangerous, trap-ridden canyon dead-ends rewards the player with hidden stockpiles containing extra lives, heavy health packs, and specialized high-tier weaponry that makes cracking the central plateau significantly easier.

Verticality & Trigger Zones:
The Visual Tripwires: Crossing specific geological thresholds within the canyon maze hits tight, invisible trigger-spawn zones embedded in the dirt paths. Hitting a zone instantly forces fresh waves of shock troops and aggressive counter-snipers to manifest on the wooden scaffolding and rocky ledges directly above the player's position, immediately tilting the vertical balance of power.

Tactical Overview:
Speed and long-range precision are your only saving graces in this sun-baked slaughterhouse. Keep your eyes locked on the shifting sands to avoid the buried minefields, watch the structural integrity of the outer ledges, and use the perimeter rifle to clear the high ground before advancing into the bottleneck. Scour the dangerous dead-ends for extra lives and heavy weapons when your supplies run low, then push up the central plateau with everything you have. Breach the tarmac, cut down Von Schatz’s remaining zealots, and claim the blood diamonds before the Reich's future takes flight.


Level 4: The Reich’s Flesh Factory                                            HOME






















Antagonist:
Oberführer Dr. Eberhardt Von Rindfleisch operates as the Director of the clandestine SS Ministerium für Menschliche Verbesserung (The Ministry of Human Improvement). A sadistic butcher hidden behind a lab coat, Von Rindfleisch viewed the human form not as sacred, but as mere raw materials waiting to be optimized through violent biochemical restructuring. When Berlin fell, any remaining ethical constraints dissolved entirely into absolute madness. Safe within his hidden laboratory, he converted his specialized knowledge into a horrific weapon, engineering the localized pandemic that transformed the battlefield into an active slaughterhouse.

Catalyst:
Serum-X7: "Fleisch-Katalysator" (The Meat Catalyst). The Ministry's crowning achievement. A highly volatile, glowing chemical compound designed to render mortality obsolete. Upon injection, the serum aggressively shuts down higher brain functions, legalizes cellular death, and violently reanimates muscle tissue—converting a living body into a mindless, homicidal, flesh engine.

Site:
Eisenwald Penitentiary (The Iron Forest). A brutalist, three-tiered concrete tomb containing exactly 128 containment cells. Von Rindfleisch commandeered the facility to conduct human trials. He started with the inmates, but decided to inject everyone, guards, staff, and even local political detainees. Now, it is a blood-splattered charnel house completely overrun by a chaotic, violent cross-section of the undead.

Situation:
The Allied high command needs cell space, and they needed it yesterday. High-ranking Nazi war criminals, architects of the Holocaust, and displaced SS generals are being rounded up across Europe. They are scheduled to stand trial and face a swift drop from a gallows rope—but we need somewhere secure to hold them while the paperwork clears. Eisenwald Penitentiary is the designated holding ground, but right now, it’s occupied by Reich augments. Von Rindfleisch's "human improvements" have completely overrun the facility. Every cell is painted with blood, and the 128 cells are packed to the with reanimated corpses that refuse to stay dead. The facility must be completely sanitized and made safe for the incoming prisoners, before they are executed.

Directives:
Infiltrate the Eisenwald prison complex. Eliminate all augmented undead assets across all three vertical tiers. Terminate any remaining rogue elements of the Ministry of Human Improvement. Clear and secure all 128 cell units to allow immediate inmate intake for the upcoming war trials.

Visuals & Atmosphere:
The Flashlight Metric: Sight lines are kept deliberately short, The player has a powerful, hard-edged flashlight active at all times. The brutalist concrete layout is a blood-splattered nightmare. Walls are painted with frantic arterial sprays, cells are smeared with rusted gore, and floor tiles are slick with fluids that catch the sharp reflection of your flashlight beam.








Audio & Soundtrack:
The darkness is filled with the mechanical wheezing of their ruined lungs, and flesh-tearing snarls echoing through the blood splattered walls.

 the concrete pipes. The volume of the zombie snarls scales sharply based on distance. The soundtrack is hard-driving, high-energy gothic industrial horror music. Heavy, distorted synthesizers, pulsing low-end basslines that mimic a racing heartbeat, and aggressive, industrial metal percussion. It isn't passive background noise; it’s a relentless sonic assault that drives the player forward, keeping the adrenaline maxed out while clearing the cells.

The Shufflers & Walkers:
Are the baseline Zombie horde. Composed of standard Inmates in tattered uniforms and Prison Guards. They move with classic, predictable gaits but use strength of numbers to corner the player in tight cells and devour him.

The Fast & Slow:
Pure kinetic variance. Some assets respond to the serum with explosive adrenaline bursts, sprinting blindly through the dark toward your flashlight beam, while others crawl or trudge at a glacial, lumbering pace, acting as bullet-sponges that block your escape routes. Reanimated SS staff and Ministry guards form the heavily armored, aggressive Nazi faction.

The dissenters:
Hippie faction consists of political prisoners, peace-advocates, and anti-war detainees swept up by the SS and tossed into Eisenwald, to be used as lab rats.


























Exploders:
Volatile, unstable augments whose internal chemistry has reached a boiling point. Shooting them at close range is suicide; they must be popped from a distance to clear out surrounding crowds or risk blowing the player sky-high.

Ragdolls:
Glitched, broken, and completely loose physical horrors. They twitch violently, tumbling over railings and moving with erratic, unpredictable physics that make landing clean headshots a frantic guessing game.

Verticality & Trigger Zones:
The Drop-In Trap: The upper tiers (Tier 2 and Tier 3) are lined with active, pacing zombies jumping down over the edge of broken concrete walkways and rusted railings to land on the player.

Invisible Ambush Triggers:
As the player advances through specific choke points on the ground floor—such as stepping into the center of the main cell block or crossing the threshold of the guard rectory—they hit invisible trigger zones. It can cause a rain of diverse undead to plunge over the railings and drop straight down into the player's immediate vicinity. You aren't just fighting what's in front of you; you have to constantly check the ceiling as the hard-driving gothic music spikes.

Logistics & Resource Management:
You are granted a near infinite pool of ammunition for your primary firearms. You don't need to waste time scrounging through empty lockers or counting bullets; you can lay down a continuous, uninterrupted wall of lead to push back the encroaching meat wave. Antidote Injectors (Health Pickups) are scattered about. When the horde breaches your perimeter and your health drops, slapping one of these injectors into your system delivers an immediate chemical counter-agent that neutralizes the infection strain and boosts your health.

High-Explosive Ordnance:
The Grenade Stockpile & Tactical Catch: You are equipped with fragmentation grenades specifically meant for crowd control. Grenades must be deployed with absolute precision. In the narrow, three-tiered concrete corridors of Eisenwald, a poorly thrown explosive will bounce off a rusted cell door or a low ceiling and land right back at your feet. However, if you use them carefully, a single well-timed toss can instantly vaporize a clustered trigger-drop or detonate an Exploder from a safe distance, triggering a massive, screen-clearing chain reaction.

Tactical Overview:
Use your flashlight to slice through the dark, track the guttural snarls bouncing off the concrete walls, and watch the walkways above you. Keep a tight grip on your grenades in these tight spaces, and look for those antidote injectors when the tiers start closing in. Paint the walls with what's left of Rindfleisch's science project and make this prison secure for the condemned fascists.







Level 5: Timberline Warzone




Lore:
The Timberline Sector is the cursed site of the Ardennes Ghost Trial of 1944, where an experimental cloaking array tore a reality rift that caused an entire division to vanish. Now a localized anomaly, the suffocating forest and shifting hills actively fight to swallow anyone who enters. The trail signs scattered through the deep grass weren't left by park rangers; they were posted by stranded survivors trying to chart the mutating woods, a warning that stepping off the marked paths means walking into a permanent ambush by Nazis of the lost division.

Woodland Environment:
Beyond the canyon trenches lies a massive, sprawling, and incredibly dense woodland map that transitions into high hills, natural boulder cover, thick foliage, and deep, neck-high grass. Winding wilderness trails run everywhere through the trees, marked by little wooden directional posts to break up the overwhelming visual chaos of the forest and aid the player with basic navigation.


Because the forest is designed to be mathematically massive and organic, it acts as a disorienting labyrinth where everything naturally starts to look identical, threatening to swallow the player's sense of direction completely.



























Deceptive Ambience:
The environmental lighting shifts dramatically into a bright, picturesque, sunny day, filled with the peaceful ambient audio of songbirds chirping in the canopy and distant guard dogs barking playfully. |


This serene atmosphere is paired with a deceptive, relaxing soundtrack mixed to induce a false sense of security in the player.  It appears to be a relaxing break from the harsh, claustrophobic, zombie-filled reality of the previous level's flesh factory. However, appearances can be fatal. This sunny wilderness hides elite military forces waiting to violently shatter the tranquil illusion.


Nonsensical Signage:
Adding to the bizarre, psychological atmosphere of the outpost, trail signs feature completely nonsensical mileage indicators that defy geographical reality. You will encounter markers pointing in random directions claiming it is five hundred miles to Paris, twelve miles to Berlin, or ten miles to Seattle, Someone’s joke or madness? .


Rather than offering real-world tracking, these contradictory signs function purely as distinct visual landmarks within the gameplay loop, allowing the player to anchor themselves in the environment. They offer a dark, surreal touch to the level design, breaking up the exhausting green and brown monotony of the deep forest while hinting at the total isolation of the base.

Enemy Camps & Resources:
German forces have carved out the wilderness to establish clusters of little outpost camps, canvas tents and camp sites. Crucial  medical tents are strategically located right in the dead-center of these enemy encampments, turning every recovery item into a high-risk objective.

This design structure aggressively forces the player to either engage in a direct frontal assault or meticulously infiltrate the heart of the camps whenever they need to heal their wounds.

You cannot simply stay on the outer perimeter and survive; you must constantly push into the lion's den to access the vital resources required to sustain your mission.


Grassland Combat Logic:
The Nazi garrison utilizes the dense natural environment to its absolute advantage, hunkering down and concealing themselves within the deep grass to set up deadly, low-profile ambushes. The highly unscripted, adaptive enemy AI refuses to follow predictable paths, meaning soldiers will either dig into deep defensive foxholes or aggressively charge your position if they spot an opening.

To help the player survive this hidden threat, the user interface features a highly responsive directional threat indicator, utilizing an outer circle and a moving center arrow that maps sound vectors and incoming muzzle flashes. Additionally, a prominent, arcade-style Nazi Kill Counter sits fixed at the top of the screen, tracking every single elimination in real-time to fuel your forward momentum.

Win Conditions:
Befitting the open, sandboxed nature of the plateau approach, the level features dual, split pathways to victory that cater to different playstyles. Players can choose to tactically navigate the brutal terrain, bypass non-essential combat zones, and find the heavily guarded, designated extraction winzone to secure a quiet escape.

Alternatively, you can achieve a total victory by engaging in absolute, systematic eradication of every fascist bastard via the Nazi Kill Counter at the top of the user interface.

Whether you slip through the trees like a ghost or leave the forest entirely cleared of the nazi threat, the choice of engagement is left entirely to your tactical discretion.









Level 6: Part 1: The Truth Extraction Center


















Facility:
Das Sanktuarium der Geisselung (The Pain-Mill Asylum) The original medieval insane asylum, known in local folklore as a place where the insane were subjected to violent physical trauma to "purge the flesh." This form of  “treatment” usually consisted of beating the devil out of patients.

Overview & Ideology:
Recently, a renegade occult order operating within the highest echelons of the SS, took over the building and repurposed it into their own little hell. Emphasizing the systematic extraction of terror, their central tenet is that human suffering is a literal conduit for spiritual energy. They reject standard military doctrines in favor of ritualistic force multiplication, merging execution apparatuses with their absolute devotion to Lucifer. Their goal is the physical breakdown of the human form via live human sacrifices, dedicated altars, to glorify their horned leader.

Key facility Personnel:
Sanktuarium Director: Hohepriester des Schwarzen Ordens (High Priest of the Black Order)
Chief of Research: Foltermeister der Schutzstaffel (SS Torture Master)
Security Commandant: Opferwart des Altars (Sacrificial Warden of the Altar)







                                                                                                                                           HOME
Echos of pain:
In a deliberate act of acoustic engineering, the order orders the systematic removal of every single interior door. This was assured that the screams of torture would reach those incarcerated, providing an auditory incentive. The facility serves as a central processing node for live human sacrifices. This implements an uninhibited acoustic environment where screams echo unimpeded through the corridors, using the permanent wall of sound to completely shatter the will of the remaining prisoners.

Because the corridors are entirely doorless, these screams bounce, overlap, and distort indefinitely, stripping away any sense of distance, direction, or safety.  The foundational soundscape is built on random, echoing ambient sounds of visceral screams.

Visuals and Environment;
The entire complex is filthy, ancient, and decrepit, with heavy blood splatters staining the walls. A thick, ominous red haze hangs over everything. The building carries a massive feeling of heaviness. It is incredibly dark, with sightlines intentionally obscured, meaning the player can only see clearly at close and medium range, keeping long-distance halls shrouded in tension.





















Aggressive Goth Horror:
Layered directly over the ambient terror is a relentless, hard-driving soundtrack.
This isn't just atmospheric drone—it is aggressive goth music laced with heavy, pounding horror themes. It features distorted basslines, piercing synths, and a driving tempo designed to heighten adrenaline and anxiety.








Disorienting Occult Broadcasts:
Striated throughout the environment, occult propaganda is violently screamed. These sudden, jarring vocalizations slice through both the music and the ambient screams, serving as chaotic audio cues that keep the listener permanently off-balance.

Mechanics and Aggression Trigger:
The entire facility is an open network of sound. The moment the player fires their weapon for the first time, the entire extraction center awakens. Every single Nazi immediately halts their bloody business and converges on the exact origin of the gunfire. Enemies are ruthless, incredibly aggressive, and will actively hunt the player down. Rather than standing still in static spawn points, they patrol and navigate along pre-defined areas throughout the facility until the trigger pulls, turning the level into a dynamic cat-and-mouse hunt, and your the mouse.

Dynamic Audio Cues:
As the player moves through different zones of the complex, specific regional audio clips trigger. These include occult audio such as chants and declarations glorifying their Lord Satan, as well as ambient horror from miscellaneous, detached screams echoing from distant, unseen rooms.

Corpse Pile:
The level features a pile of discarded corpses illuminated by a green light. Getting directly into the corpses triggers a unique mechanic. Corpses bring gifts, serving as a hidden, high-risk interaction point for the player to gain critical resources.

Courtyard:
Situated  in the middle of the torture facility is an open-air courtyard filled with beautiful flowers and pristine trees. It stands as a jarring, beautiful, and stark contrast to the grim ugliness of the indoor corridors. Exploring this deceptive safe haven yields supplies, including extra lives, standard health packs, and more. And, of course, your favorite nazis are there to be exterminated.

Level 6 Part 2: The Meat Grinder                                                              
















Deep within the bowels of the godless truth extraction centre, (A.K.A demonic torture dungeon) lies a fast and brutal gauntlet known as The Meat Grinder. The player encounters this separate section while moving through the torture dungeon. After some time in the dungeon,  the level opens directly into the slaughterhouse chaos of the grand grinder.

Environment & Layout:
The meat grinder area consists of two massive rooms, each the size of a football field and soaring six stories tall. The space is heavily choked with thirty German Panzer tanks parked at haphazard angles, their rusted hulls serving as heavy tactical cover and breaking up vital sightlines. Woven around these steel behemoths is a surreal, chronological graveyard of scattered assets.

The corpse pile:
Against the far concrete wall, a macabre pile of civilian corpses is stacked high, completely out of place. Bolted directly to the masonry right next to the pile is a row of three 1970s-style US public telephone booths— Just in case the dead desire to phone to heaven since the meatgrinder is their hell.
Corpses bring gifts: Get in the pile!


The Trap (Quad-Trigger Spawn System)
Right at the entrance to the meat grinder rooms, the player unknowingly crosses four separate trigger zones simultaneously, immediately starting four independent countdown timers.




Enemy waves:
Enemies are set to spawn in distinct intervals across these timers (e.g., Wave 1: 15–30 seconds, Wave 2: 40–60 seconds, Wave 3: 60–90 seconds) ect…All enemies spawn on the ceiling and drop down into the arena. Because the darkness restricts the view, the player cannot see most of  the assets "pop in" out of thin air, creating a seamless, sudden ambush from above.

Resources & Weapons:
The arena is scattered with standard weapons, extra lives, and health packs. A specialized shotgun is hidden within the room that can instantly wipe out swathes of enemies. However, because certain enemy variants move incredibly fast, even this weapon cannot guarantee survival.

Corpse Pile Mechanics & Lore:
At the far end of the room lies a massive, grotesque pile of corpses. The pile is bathed in a distinct green light, which consistently indicates a healing zone throughout the game. To ensure the player understands the mechanic, a large, prominent reader board points directly to the pile with the text: "The room is cold, but the bodies are warm. Corpses bring gifts. Get in the pile." Players must physically enter the corpse pile to receive presents from the dead.

Level 7: SS School of Juvenile Domination                                             HOME           

Lore:
Set in the shadowy outskirts of Wewelsburg, the SS-Schule für Jugendbeherrschung (SS School of Juvenile Domination) operated as a deeply classified, experimental facility designed to break and rebuild the minds of the regime's youth. Officially designated under the cover name Institut für Höhere Rassenpädagogik (Institute for Higher Racial Pedagogy), the fortress-like campus forced its cadets through a brutal curriculum of psychological warfare, absolute obedience, and tactical cruelty. Under the strict eye of SS leadership, instructors utilized Pavlovian conditioning and high-stakes survival trials to strip away individual empathy, transforming children into cold, fanatical tools of total state control.





Environment & Layout:
The level opens in a starting corridor where you grab weapons before entering the front door of the school. The main layout centers around six massive classrooms in the middle of the facility. Surrounding these classrooms are corridors containing offices for the different teachers.

Visuals & Lighting:
The lighting is normal, but visibility is heavily restricted by Our signature black fog obscuring the distance, creating tight, limited sight lines. Often the only indication of an enemy shooting

Tips & tricks to prevent early death!                                               HOME


1. Automatic weapon swap can cause quick death

Concept:
Subverting standard inventory expectations by turning weapon pickups into active environmental hazards that exploit traditional muscle memory. When a player carelessly runs over a low-tier weapon scattered across the arena floor, the engine automatically triggers a priority override that forces an instant pickup, immediately unequipping their high-powered primary weapon and replacing it with a completely low-grade substitute—typically an unreliable tool with an abysmal cyclic fire rate, a horrific reload speed, or a severely limited magazine capacity that offers zero crowd control.

Logic:
Upon crossing the item's precise 3D collision boundary, the internal inventory script forces an instant weapon-state swap, stripping away the active power weapon and jamming the garbage-tier firearm directly into the player's hands.

While the player can technically press the appropriate number row key to switch back to their primary weapon, the unholster animation delay and draw-time overhead during an intense, high-velocity firefight creates a fatal, un-cancelable vulnerability window.

By the time they realize their mistake and process that they are holding a useless pea-shooter, enemy combatants have already closed the distance and shredded their health pool. Learn or get burned!

Setup:
This mechanic specifically targets high-speed speedrunners, bunny-hoppers, and reckless players who rely on automated pathing and muscle memory to vacuum up every piece of floor resources without analyzing the environment. By weaponizing the physical layout of the level, it forces these aggressive players to break their forward momentum, slow down their routing, and actively assess the specific visual models of the loot scattered across the floor before moving over them.

Player Impact:
Teaches the player that in a "no-holds-barred" environment, even the resources are rigged against them and the arena itself is a hostile entity designed to actively sabotage their run. The core takeaway is brutal: master your weapon-switching hotkeys under pressure to recover from forced animation locks, or pay with your life.







2. Healing Zones can be Death Zones:


Concept:
Brutally subverting the classic "Safe space” found in traditional shooters. In most games, a glowing health station is a sigh of relief; here, it can be actively used as bait.

Setup:
High-visibility green healing zones are strategically placed in areas that seem safe but are subtly surrounded by concealed proximity mines or cloaked explosive hazards.

Kill:
When a player is critically low on health, panic sets in. They spot the glowing green sanctuary and immediately sprint toward it without scanning the perimeter. The moment they cross the threshold without checking their corners, they detonate the mines. Most mines can be easily spotted, but moving att such a high rate of speed makes it impossible to stop quickly. Low on health already, you soon die.











Player Impact:
Eliminates brainless rushing. It instills a sense of paranoia, forcing players to methodically clear the immediate environment and check for traps even when—and especially when—they are desperate for health.


3. Reload Death Loops-

Concept:
Punishing sloppy ammo management and panic-induced reloading habits.

Setup:
This logic is hardcoded into the core player loop and triggers dynamically across any active combat zone upon player respawn.

Logic:
If a player burns through an entire magazine in a panic right before dying, they will respawn directly back into the fray with that exact same empty magazine. Inherently, their first instinct is to hit the reload key and attempt to sprint away from the surrounding enemies. However, initiating a reload triggers a penalty that cuts sprinting speed exactly in half. Caught out in the open with slow movement,
the player is effortlessly torn to shreds again.

Player Impact:
Breaking this habit requires a complete mental shift: sprint to cover first, break line of sight, and only then commit to a reload. It shifts the gameplay away from mindless spraying toward strict, tactical ammo conservation. You can’t both sprint at full speed and reload at the same time!


4. Silent High-Speed Stalkers-

Concept:
Eliminating audio-based hand-holding. Modern shooters rely heavily on loud footstep cues to warn players of approaching threats; this mechanic strips away that auditory crutch entirely.

Setup:
Custom AI entities are configured with massively boosted movement speed variables and completely wiped audio profiles.











Logic:
Within the FPE (Functional Profile Editor), the entity's soundset is mapped to "null" or pointed to a completely silent audio file, rendering their movement entirely noiseless. Furthermore, a custom Lua script continuously calculates the player’s look-angle relative to the stalker's position. The stalker is programmed to freeze or slow down when checked, but the millisecond the player's look-angle shifts beyond 90 degrees away, the entity surges forward at maximum velocity.

Player Impact:
Players can no longer rely on sound cues to keep them safe. It demands absolute visual vigilance, forcing players to constantly check their six and clear rooms with their eyes
rather than their ears.





5. Reactive Backtrack Triggers-

Concept:
Eliminating the "Coward’s Retreat" tactic. This mechanic is designed to stop players from continuously avoiding high-threat forward enemies by moving backward into previously cleared, safe territories.










Logic:
Once the player crosses the forward threshold into a combat arena, the script flags the trailing "Backtrack Zone" as active. If the player panics and attempts to reverse their x/y/z coordinates back through that zone to escape the fight, the engine intercepts the movement and executes a spawn at waypoint command.  This instantly drops a heavy squad of enemy reinforcements directly into their intended escape route, boxing them in. Not all levels have this nice “feature”,  just the selected ones.

Setup:
Invisible, conditional Trigger Zones are embedded into the map layout directly behind key choke points and arenas.

Player Impact:
Commits the player entirely to the current engagement. It forces them to push forward through the chaos rather than exploiting the map geometry to cheese the AI.


6. Heal zones can be enemy magnets-                                                          HOME

Concept:
Stripping away the power of healing zones by allowing the AI to actively counter them. Heal zones are not areas of total invincibility, they can be defeated.

Setup:
Dynamic behavior scripts assigned to aggressive close-quarters AI units.

Logic:
A green lighted health zone does not guarantee safety because the enemy factions actively manipulate it. Rush-tier enemies will aggressively charge your position, using melee attacks to physically knock your character out of the healing radius so their squadmates can finish you off. Other suicidal variants will sprint directly into the zone and detonate their explosive vests, forcing you out of the radius or killing you instantly. Furthermore, the healing tick rate is finite—if a full squad coordinates fire on your position, the attack will completely overwhelm the health regeneration rate, leading to a quick death.

Player Impact:
Re-emphasizes that no space is sacred. A healing zone is merely a temporary logistical tool, not a fortress, and standing still inside one, for too long, can also be a death sentence.











7. Bullets Have Consequences:

Concept:
Simulating the oppressive weight of a real firefight by penalizing active suppression and rewarding initiative. When you are taking damage, your reaction time is slowed. If you're taking a lot of damage your reaction time is slowed even more. Just like in real life.

Setup:
A global, hardcoded combat modifier applied to both the player and the AI entities based upon initiative.

Logic:
Taking damage severely impairs mechanical performance. When a combatant is actively receiving fire, a kinetic slowdown modifier is applied, drastically reducing aiming sensitivity, prolonging reload animations, and slowing down general physical movement.

When hot lead is ripping through flesh, a coherent tactical response becomes incredibly difficult. In real combat, the entity that seizes the initiative and lands the first shot is statistically the one that survives. This is a strict two-way street: if you get ambushed, you will likely die quickly; but if you get the jump on the enemy, they die quickly

Player Impact:
Strongly demands aggressive, proactive engagement. It rewards high-precision opening strikes and severely punishes sloppy, reactive playstyles.

Concept:
The absolute eradication of modern, passive health-regeneration systems that reward failure.

9. Real Weapon Physics: all guns have recoil & muzzle rise:

It is brutal, chaotic and permanent. That is the cold, hard reality. We aimed to capture the spirit of that reality: ambushes, pressure, uncertainty, and no-win situations without artificial protection.









How the video game sausage is truly made:

Some games hide bad design behind difficulty curves. They build one level that is impossible to win,
which effectively prevents access to the other levels, and the other levels are crap. Players don’t
advance; they quit. Mission accomplished—for the wrong side: the side of cowards, without honor.

We build levels that are both accessible and brutal. No excuses, no scams, just traditional retro FPS action: kill or be killed. Learn or die.

Our game launcher lets you play every level: nothing is locked! It is made to be played in sequential order, but you are not forced into any order. Save any level at any time! You pick the mission and deploy. But remember- you can’t heal damage hiding behind cover for 5 seconds.









Operation Obliteration 2: The free demo

The internet is drowning in low-effort games. Asset flips. Shovelware. Mass-produced junk is pushed out as fast as possible in the hope that something sticks and makes a buck. One thing these games almost always avoid: a free demo. Why? Because the moment players try them, the illusion collapses. If the game’s a steaming pile of fresh steer excrement, nobody’s buying it. We do the opposite. We offer a free demo on purpose—not to hide behind marketing, but to stand out from the noise. If our game holds up, the demo will prove it. And it does!


Our demo opens with a clear, no-nonsense warning: get to the shooting range and get some guns.
This is not flavor text. This is survival advice. Ignore it and the game will later correct you—violently.
When you reach the range, you’ll find something most games would never dare to show up front:
every weapon in the game, laid out on a table, ready for inspection.

No stupid loot boxes. No mystery.

Pick them up. Fire them. Learn how they behave. Just remember—you only have nine inventory
slots, so choices matter. You can’t carry everything, and bad loadouts have consequences.






























Nearby is the shooting gallery. It’s wired with high explosives and packed with Nazi pig targets designed
to react in loud, messy and deeply satisfying ways. You’ll also notice captured augments present. What
you do with that situation is entirely up to you.

We give you the tools, this is not a morality lecture.

At some point—without ceremony and without warning—you’ll encounter a squad of fascist zombies
attempting to eat your face for lunch.

Let’s be very clear: this is not a zombie horror game. Zombies appear only in the demo and in Level 4 of Operation Obliteration 2. Think of them as a stress test, not a genre shift.

Zombies are traditional in most FPS shooters… War gets strange. Deal with it.

As the demo continues, additional “friends” will arrive to join the party. They’re aggressive. They’re
persistent. Your new friends want to play, we suggest you don’t disappoint them!

What you’re playing is not a tutorial in the modern, padded sense. It’s a live-fire exercise—a small but
honest slice of our unhinged WWII universe. The world does not pause for you. Triggers stack. Timers
run. Escalation is inevitable. Just like real combat, the situation evolves whether you’re ready or not.



























This demo exists for one reason: to prove the game stands on its own. No smoke. No mirrors. No cinematic excuses. If you survive it, you’ll understand exactly what kind of game this is—and whether you’re ready for the rest of the war. You are placed into hostile conditions and are expected to adapt or die. The enemy can be defeated.

Every level can be cleared. But only if you act correctly. Your mistakes will be punished.

Death will be your greatest teacher. Each death teaches you something the UI never will. What triggered what. What you ignored. What you should have prepared for five minutes earlier.
Every level in the game can be beaten. You are always given the means to defeat every enemy. The catch is that the solution isn’t labeled, highlighted, or spoon-fed.

Players are given flexible tools and multiple approaches to combat, encouraging adaptability rather than a single correct path. Systems are designed to interact dynamically, creating encounters that feel unpredictable while remaining fair. Levels are constructed to reward observation and problem-solving, with challenges that can be overcome through preparation, positioning, and smart use of resources.

The project draws inspiration from classic first-person shooters, prioritizing responsive controls, easily readable environments, and gameplay-driven tension. The result is a focused, systems-driven experience that values player skill, discovery, and replayability. No hand-holding. No gimmicks.
Just systems built to be learned, consequences that matter, and choices that stick.
This isn’t call of duty!
